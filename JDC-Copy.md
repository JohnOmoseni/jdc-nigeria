## Copy Control — Public Positioning and Navigation

The page must read as a serious digital-infrastructure development platform: partner-led, operator-grade and commercially confident, while remaining exact about what is still under development.

### Controlling public position

- Public name: JDC Nigeria Data Centres. Use "JDC (Nigeria) SPV Ltd" only in legal or footer references.
- Location: Southern Nigeria. Do not name Osisioma, Aba, AMIC or Ogboji on the public site at this stage.
- Lead with 55 MW facility load. Do not headline a fixed IT load or PUE.
- State 60 MW installed generation separately from the 55 MW facility requirement.
- Use "Tier III-targeted design," not "Tier III certified" or "Tier III ready."
- Opening 2029 remains a prominent development statement.
- No named operator, carrier, customer or investor logos until participation is documented.
- Do not mention "JEX" anywhere.

### Recommended navigation

Campus • Infrastructure • Services • Partnerships • Why Southern Nigeria • Contact

**Design note:** Transparent navigation over the hero, becoming solid navy on scroll. The primary navigation button should read "Discuss a Partnership."

### Brand and visual direction

- Deep navy foundation with electric cyan and signal teal; use warm amber only as a single accent node or emphasis colour.
- Modern, technical and operator-grade. Avoid startup language, generic AI hype and dense small text.
- Use the supplied JDC Pulse Grid logo without generative redrawing. Rebuild the page as editable responsive components, not as one long image.
- Preserve the existing mockup's cinematic infrastructure imagery and strong white-to-dark section rhythm.

## Build With JDC (Partnerships)

**Headline:** A platform designed for the right partners.

JDC is assembling the specialist capability required to design, deliver, operate and commercialise a major data-centre platform in Southern Nigeria. Participation is reserved for partners that bring measurable long-term value.

**On-page copy:** Up to 25% of JDC (Nigeria) SPV Ltd is reserved collectively for selected specialist operating, technical, connectivity and commercial partners. Participation will reflect measurable contribution — not name recognition alone.

**Four partner categories:**

1. **Operating and certification partners** — Lead operator design standards, commissioning, Tier certification pathways, operating systems, staffing and long-term facility management.
2. **Technology and delivery partners** — Bring data-centre engineering, cooling, electrical systems, controls, security, construction management and integrated commissioning capability.
3. **Connectivity and ecosystem partners** — Provide diverse fibre routes, carrier-neutral interconnection, cloud and content access, internet exchange connectivity and customer reach.
4. **Commercial and customer partners** — Support anchor capacity, customer origination, enterprise access, market development and long-term contracted business.

**What JDC values:** Delivery capability · Operating excellence · Customer access · Technology · Long-term commitment

**Design note:** This is the most prominent body section after the hero. Use four spacious partner cards and a strong "Discuss a Partnership" CTA. **Do not show unconfirmed company names or logos.**

## The Campus

**Headline:** 55 MW of facility scale. Modular from the ground up.

JDC is being developed as one fully coordinated campus platform. The common power, connectivity, security, roads, drainage and facility infrastructure will be designed for the full programme, while data halls and white space are activated in commercially sensible modules aligned with customer commitments.

Final saleable IT capacity will be established through operator-led design, reflecting cooling architecture, rack density, resilience and the requirements of cloud, enterprise and high-density workloads.

- Modular data halls and private suites
- High-density and AI/HPC-ready design pathways
- Scalable electrical and cooling distribution
- Integrated fire, security and monitoring systems
- Customer activation aligned with contracted demand

**Design note:** Use a campus plan, modular-hall graphic or architectural cutaway. **Avoid displaying a fixed IT-capacity number.**

The development is designed around 60 MW of installed generation delivered by Wellsmart Energy Limited. A modular N+1 architecture, integrated ride-through systems, black-start capability and emergency resilience will be engineered around the operating requirements of the data-centre campus.

## Services

**Headline:** Capacity for the workloads shaping the next decade.

JDC will support customers from single enterprise deployments to dedicated halls and high-density environments. Commercial structures will be developed around long-term, USD-denominated capacity and service agreements for qualifying customers.

1. **Wholesale Colocation** — Scalable capacity for cloud, content, carrier and large enterprise customers requiring contiguous space and dependable infrastructure.
2. **Enterprise Private Suites** — Secure, dedicated environments for regulated institutions, industrial companies, government and critical business systems.
3. **Hyperscale and Cloud** — Modular campus capacity designed to support cloud platforms, content providers and large-scale digital services.
4. **AI and High-Performance Computing** — Power and cooling pathways designed for high-density deployments, subject to final operator-led engineering.
5. **Disaster Recovery and Business Continuity** — Location-diverse infrastructure for organisations strengthening resilience beyond their primary operating environments.
6. **Managed Connectivity and Cross-Connects** — Carrier access, interconnection, cloud on-ramps, cross-connects and related operational support.

**Design note:** Use a clean 3 × 2 card grid on desktop and stacked cards on mobile. Keep service text short and readable.

## Why Southern Nigeria

**Headline:** Digital growth needs infrastructure beyond one market.

Southern Nigeria brings together major population centres, financial institutions, industrial clusters, public-sector workloads and regional businesses that require resilient local infrastructure and meaningful location diversity.

- A growing enterprise and industrial demand base
- Location diversification beyond Lagos
- Onshore data, resilience and business-continuity requirements
- A platform for regional cloud, content and connectivity growth
