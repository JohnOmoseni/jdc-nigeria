## DESIGN GUIDE

### Visual direction

- Deep navy foundation with electric cyan and signal teal. Use warm amber only as a restrained accent.
- Modern, technical and operator-grade. Avoid generic startup styling and exaggerated AI imagery.
- Use the supplied JDC Pulse Grid logo without redrawing it.
- Build the page as editable, responsive components rather than one long image.
- Use cinematic infrastructure imagery and a strong white-to-dark section rhythm.
- Prioritise large, readable type and generous spacing across desktop and mobile.

### Navigation and hero

- Use transparent navigation over the hero, changing to solid navy on scroll.
- Keep "Discuss a Partnership" as the primary navigation button.
- Retain a campus-at-dusk hero image.
- Give "Powered by Partnership" the cyan/teal highlight treatment.
- Present the four hero metrics in a clean, highly legible horizontal strip on desktop and stacked cards on mobile.

### Section treatments

- From Power to Packet: use a simple sequence — Energy → Facility → Connectivity → Operations → Customer Experience.
- Build with JDC: use four spacious partner cards and a prominent partnership CTA.
- The Campus: use a campus plan, modular-hall graphic or architectural cutaway.
- Power and Resilience: visualise continuity, modularity and N+1 resilience rather than fuel infrastructure.
- Connectivity: use a clean network visual with two diverse route paths and carrier-neutral interconnection.
- Services: use a three-by-two card grid on desktop and stacked cards on mobile.
- Why Southern Nigeria: use an abstract regional network graphic without an exact campus marker.
- Sustainability: use restrained engineering and efficiency imagery.
- Contact: keep both partnership and capacity calls to action visible above the form.

### Responsive behaviour

- Keep headline line lengths short and preserve the hero hierarchy on small screens.
- Stack metrics, partner cards and service cards cleanly on mobile.
- Maintain strong contrast and minimum readable text sizes throughout.
- Keep CTA buttons full-width or comfortably tappable on mobile.
- Use consistent spacing between sections and avoid dense text blocks.
